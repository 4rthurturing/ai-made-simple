3. Content Calendar (12 Weeks)

Week 1: How to Use ChatGPT: A Step-by-Step Guide for Complete Beginners

Keywords: how to use ChatGPT, ChatGPT for seniors, ChatGPT beginners guide • 1,500-1,800 words

Walk through creating an account, typing your first question, and understanding the response. Include screenshots. Cover the free version only. Mention what NOT to type (personal details, bank info).

Week 2: 10 Useful Things You Can Ask ChatGPT Today

Keywords: what can ChatGPT do, ChatGPT prompts for seniors, practical uses of ChatGPT • 1,200-1,500 words

Give ten specific, practical examples: writing a letter, planning a meal, explaining a medical term, finding a recipe, composing a birthday message. Each with the exact prompt to copy.

Week 3: AI Scams: How to Spot a Fake Voice Call

Keywords: AI voice scams, deepfake phone call, how to spot AI scam • 1,200-1,500 words

Explain how voice cloning works in plain terms. Give real examples of the "grandparent scam." List red flags and what to do if you get a suspicious call. Include Action Fraud contact details.

Week 4: What is a Chatbot? The Plain English Guide

Keywords: what is a chatbot, chatbot explained, difference between chatbot and AI • 1,000-1,200 words

Explain what chatbots are, where people encounter them (bank websites, customer service), how they differ from full AI like ChatGPT, and when to trust them vs when to ask for a human.

Week 5: How AI is Already Helping the NHS

Keywords: AI NHS, artificial intelligence healthcare UK, AI and your GP • 1,200-1,500 words

Cover AI in radiology, the NHS App, triage chatbots, and how AI helps with waiting lists. Reassure readers that AI assists doctors rather than replacing them. UK-specific throughout.

Week 6: How to Spot a Deepfake Photo or Video

Keywords: how to spot a deepfake, fake AI images, deepfake detection • 1,000-1,200 words

Explain what deepfakes are with examples. Show common tells (hands, teeth, background details). Explain why they matter (misinformation, scams). Keep it practical and not scary.

Week 7: Using AI to Help With Your Hobbies

Keywords: AI for gardening, AI for cooking, AI practical uses everyday • 1,500-1,800 words

Show how to use ChatGPT or Google for gardening advice, recipe ideas, genealogy research, knitting patterns, and travel planning. Each hobby gets a worked example with screenshots.

Week 8: What Should You Never Tell ChatGPT? A Safety Guide

Keywords: what not to tell ChatGPT, ChatGPT privacy, is ChatGPT safe to use • 1,000-1,200 words

Explain what data ChatGPT stores. List specific things to avoid sharing: full name + address, bank details, NHS number, passwords. Explain why in simple terms. Offer safer alternatives.

Week 9: AI and Loneliness: Can a Chatbot Be a Companion?

Keywords: AI help with loneliness, AI companion elderly, chatbot for companionship • 1,200-1,500 words

Explore AI companions honestly. Cover what they can and cannot do. Mention ElliQ and similar products. Be balanced: acknowledge benefits while stressing that AI is not a replacement for human contact.

Week 10: Free AI Courses and Resources for Over-60s in the UK

Keywords: free AI courses seniors UK, learn AI free, AI classes for older adults • 1,000-1,200 words

Curate a list of genuinely free resources: Senior Planet, Elements of AI, BBC guides, local library courses, Age UK digital skills. Include direct links and brief descriptions of each.

Week 11: A Grandparent's Guide to the AI Your Grandchildren Use

Keywords: AI children use, explain AI to grandparents, AI apps young people • 1,200-1,500 words

Cover Snapchat AI, TikTok algorithms, AI in gaming, AI homework tools. Help grandparents understand what their grandchildren are doing with AI so they can have informed conversations.

Week 12: AI Jargon Buster: 20 Terms Explained in Plain English

Keywords: AI glossary, AI terms explained, what does LLM mean • 1,500-1,800 words

Expand on the existing glossary with 20 key terms: algorithm, machine learning, neural network, LLM, generative AI, prompt, hallucination, deepfake, chatbot, etc. One paragraph each, no jargon in the definitions.

5. Technical SEO Recommendations

Site Speed

  • Compress all images to WebP format (under 100KB each)
  • Enable Next.js image optimisation with next/image
  • Use static generation (SSG) for all content pages
  • Set Cache-Control headers for static assets
  • Audit bundle size with next build

Mobile Optimisation

  • Test on real devices (iPads, older Android phones)
  • Minimum tap target size: 48x48px
  • Body text minimum 18px (larger text for our audience)
  • Test with VoiceOver and TalkBack screen readers

Schema Markup

  • Add WebSite schema to homepage
  • Add Article schema to every guide/blog page
  • Add DefinedTermSet schema to glossary
  • Add FAQPage schema to pages with Q&A sections (wins featured snippets)

Sitemap and Robots

  • Generate sitemap.xml automatically via Next.js app/sitemap.ts
  • Submit to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools
  • Create public/robots.txt allowing all crawlers, blocking /api/

7. Content Writing Guidelines

Article Structure

  1. Opening paragraph (2-3 sentences) - State what the article covers and why it matters. Include the primary keyword naturally.
  2. Key takeaway box - A highlighted box with the main point in one sentence.
  3. Body sections - H2 for main sections, H3 for subsections. Paragraphs of 2-4 sentences max.
  4. Practical examples - Every concept needs a real-world example.
  5. Safety note (where relevant) - A boxed reminder.
  6. What to do next - Clear call to action linking to another Sage page.

Word Counts

Content TypeTarget
Core guide pages1,200-1,800 words
Blog posts1,000-1,500 words
Glossary entries80-150 words each
Quick tips400-600 words

Keyword Usage

  • Include the primary keyword in the first 100 words
  • Use it in at least one H2 heading
  • Sprinkle related terms (synonyms, variations) throughout
  • Write for the reader first; if a sentence sounds odd with the keyword, rewrite it
  • Use question keywords as H2/H3 headings (e.g., "Is AI safe to use?")

Internal Linking Rules

  • Every article links to at least 2 other Sage pages
  • Descriptive anchor text only. Good: "read our guide to staying safe with AI." Bad: "click here."
  • New posts link back to relevant core pages
  • Update older articles to link to newer content when published

Writing Style Rules (Important!)

  • No em dashes. Use commas, full stops, or brackets instead.
  • British English throughout. Colour, organisation, programme (but "program" for software), realise, centre.
  • No AI writing tells. Avoid: "In today's rapidly evolving landscape," "It's important to note that," "Let's dive in," "In conclusion," "Here's the thing," "Navigating the world of."
  • Short sentences. 20 words max where possible.
  • Active voice. "AI learns from data" not "Data is learned from by AI."
  • Second person. Write "you" not "one" or "the user."
  • Contractions are fine. "It's," "you'll," "don't."
  • Avoid hedging. "AI can help with cooking" not "AI may potentially be able to assist with cooking activities."
  • Be specific. "Ask ChatGPT to write a birthday message for your granddaughter" not "Use AI for various communication purposes."
  • Read it aloud. If it sounds like a robot wrote it, rewrite it.
