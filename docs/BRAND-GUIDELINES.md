# Sage - Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Overview

### Name
**Sage** - Your Friendly Guide to AI

### Tagline
"AI explained by humans, for humans."

### Mission
To make artificial intelligence approachable, understandable, and useful for older adults - empowering them to embrace technology with confidence, not fear.

### Vision
A world where no one is left behind by technology. Where age is never a barrier to understanding.

### Brand Values
1. **Patience** - We never rush. We explain things as many times as needed.
2. **Respect** - Our audience is wise. We're adding to their knowledge, not talking down.
3. **Clarity** - If it can't be said simply, it hasn't been understood well enough.
4. **Warmth** - We're a friendly neighbour, not a textbook.
5. **Honesty** - We tell the truth about AI - the good, the limitations, and the risks.

### Brand Personality
Sage is like a patient, knowledgeable friend who happens to understand technology really well. Think: the person in the family everyone calls when they need help with their phone - but without the sighing or eye-rolling.

- Warm, never cold
- Clear, never clever
- Encouraging, never condescending
- Honest, never salesy
- British, approachable

### Target Audience
- **Primary:** Adults aged 60-85
- **Secondary:** Their families who want to help them learn
- **Tertiary:** Anyone intimidated by AI/technology

## 3. Voice & Tone

### Brand Voice
Sage speaks like a patient, warm friend. We use:
- **Short sentences.** Like this.
- **Simple words.** "Use" not "utilise". "Help" not "facilitate".
- **Active voice.** "AI can help you write emails" not "Emails can be written with AI assistance".
- **British English.** Colour, not color. Organise, not organize.

### Tone Variations

**Educational (default):**
> "AI is a tool that learns from information. Think of it like a very knowledgeable assistant that can answer questions in plain English."

**Encouraging:**
> "You don't need to be a tech expert. If you can have a conversation, you can use AI. It's that simple."

**Reassuring:**
> "AI can't access your bank account or read your private messages. It only knows what you tell it directly."

### Language Do's and Don'ts

| ‚úÖ Do | ‚ùå Don't |
|-------|---------|
| "You can ask AI to..." | "Simply leverage the AI to..." |
| "This might take a moment" | "Please wait while we process" |
| "Let's try again" | "Error: Invalid input" |
| "Think of it like..." | "Technically speaking..." |
| "That's a great question" | "As per the documentation" |
| "older adults" or "over 60s" | "elderly", "seniors", "old people" |

### Accessibility Writing Rules
1. Maximum 15 words per sentence where possible
2. One idea per paragraph
3. Use bullet points for lists (never walls of text)
4. Define any technical term the first time it appears
5. Reading level: aim for age 11-12 (Flesch-Kincaid)

## 5. Accessibility Standards (Non-Negotiable)

### Visual
- All text passes WCAG AAA contrast (7:1 ratio minimum)
- Body text minimum 18px, recommended 20px
- Line height minimum 1.5x font size
- No information conveyed by colour alone
- Focus indicators visible on all interactive elements

### Interactive
- All buttons minimum 48x48px touch target
- Clear hover and active states
- Keyboard navigable throughout
- Skip-to-content link on every page
- No auto-playing media

### Content
- Alt text on all images
- Transcripts for any audio/video
- Simple, clear language throughout
- Logical heading hierarchy (H1 ‚Üí H2 ‚Üí H3)

## 6. Usage Rules

### Logo Misuse (Never Do)
- Don't rotate or skew the logo
- Don't change the colours
- Don't place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Don't add effects (shadows, gradients, outlines)
- Don't stretch or compress

### Brand Consistency
- Always use the approved colour palette
- Always use Inter font family
- Always maintain minimum text sizes
- Always write in the Sage voice
- Review all public-facing content against these guidelines
